What to watch!!

Bring her back

Bring Her Back (2025) is a horror-drama about two foster siblings placed with a grieving woman who secretly uses occult rituals to bring back her dead daughter—forcing the children to fight for survival.

review: 7.45/10

Bring Her Back is really creepy in a slow, unsettling way. Sally Hawkins is amazing she feels caring one moment and terrifying the next. The sibling story adds real heart, which makes the scary parts hit harder. It’s not packed with jump scares, but it leaves you thinking about it long after it’s over.

The pope's exorcist

The Pope’s Exorcist (2023) follows Father Amorth, the Vatican’s chief exorcist, as he confronts a terrifying possession that uncovers a hidden Church secret.

review: 8.02/10

The Pope’s Exorcist is a tense and entertaining horror with Russell Crowe bringing a lot of personality to Father Amorth. It mixes classic possession scares with a bigger mystery, so it feels both creepy and adventurous. It’s not the scariest exorcism movie out there, but it’s fun, stylish, and surprisingly engaging.

Until dawn

Until Dawn is a horror story where a group of friends spend the night in a remote mountain lodge, only to face a masked killer and terrifying creatures—forcing them to make life-or-death choices until morning.

review: 5.68/10

Until Dawn feels like playing through a horror movie you control a group of friends stuck on a mountain where every choice can mean life or death. It’s tense, full of twists, and the choices really make you feel the pressure. It mixes slasher vibes with supernatural horror, and while some parts are a bit over the top, it’s a thrilling experience that keeps you on edge.

Evil dead rise

Evil Dead Rise (2023) is a horror film where two estranged sisters must fight for survival after flesh-possessing demons are unleashed in a Los Angeles apartment building.

review: 8.50/10

Evil Dead Rise is brutal, bloody, and relentless from start to finish. It takes the classic Evil Dead chaos out of the cabin and into a city apartment, which makes it feel fresh but still terrifying. The gore is extreme, the scares are intense, and the family dynamic adds real emotional weight. It’s not for the faint of heart, but if you love hardcore horror, it delivers.

The conjuring annabelle

A young couple’s home is invaded by a malevolent force after their new doll, Annabelle, becomes possessed. The entity relentlessly targets them and their unborn child.

review: 7.26/10

It’s creepy and gives you that uneasy feeling, but it’s kinda slow. Some scares are predictable, but the vibe is spooky and the doll is just uncomfortable to look at. Not the scariest Conjuring movie, but still worth watching if you like creepy horror.

Annabelle creation

Years before the original Annabelle, a grieving dollmaker creates the doll that becomes possessed. A group of orphans in his care begin experiencing terrifying supernatural events.

review: 7.87/10

This one is actually scary. Way better than the first Annabelle, with real tension and creepy scenes that stick with you. It still has a slow build, but the scares land harder and the overall vibe is much darker. Definitely one of the stronger movies in the Conjuring universe.

Annabelle comes home

Annabelle is locked in the Warrens’ artifact room, but when it’s accidentally released, evil spirits escape and terrorize a babysitter and her friends in the Warren house.

review: 6.98/10

This one feels more fun than scary. It has a bunch of creepy moments and cool ideas, but it’s less intense and more like a haunted house movie. Not as scary as *Annabelle: Creation*, but still entertaining and easy to watch if you like the Conjuring universe.

Insidious

A family’s young son falls into a mysterious coma and becomes a magnet for dark spirits in a shadowy dimension called “The Further.” They call on a psychic to help save him before the spirits claim his soul.

review: 8.48/10

This movie is actually scary. It has super creepy vibes, loud jump scares, and scenes that stick in your head after it ends. The story gets a little weird, but in a cool way. Definitely one of those horror movies that makes you scared of the dark afterward.

Insidious 2

The haunting continues as the family uncovers lingering supernatural forces and hidden secrets about the father’s past. They fight to protect their son and confront a malevolent entity.

review: 8.12/10

Not as scary as the first one, but still creepy. It leans more into the story and explains things instead of nonstop scares. Some moments are tense and unsettling, but it feels a bit slower. Still good if you liked the first Insidious and want more of the story.

Insidious 3

A prequel focusing on psychic Elise Rainier as she helps a teenage girl haunted by a vengeful spirit, showing the dangers of connecting with the dead.

review: 5.32/10

This one’s pretty creepy and tense, with some really unsettling moments. It’s more about building suspense than nonstop scares, so it can feel a bit slow sometimes. Still a solid horror movie if you like spooky vibes and a darker story.

Insidious the last key

Elise returns to her childhood home to face a powerful, malevolent entity tied to her past. She must confront her own trauma while battling supernatural forces.

review: 8.65/10

This one’s genuinely creepy and keeps you on edge, with some scenes that stick with you. It’s more about building tension than nonstop scares, so it can feel slow at times, but the story is pretty interesting and gives the characters more depth. Overall, it’s a solid, spooky watch.

Insidious the red door

Years later, the Lambert family revisits “The Further” to face unresolved trauma. A dangerous entity threatens their family, forcing them to confront past demons once and for all.

review: 8.78/10

This one’s intense and creepy, with a lot of tense moments that actually make you jump. The story digs deeper into the characters, which makes it more engaging than just a bunch of scares. Some parts drag a bit, but the mood and suspense are strong, making it a solid horror watch.

The conjuring

Paranormal investigators Ed and Lorraine Warren help the Perron family, who are being terrorized by a dark spirit in their farmhouse. As the haunting grows, they uncover a terrifying connection to witchcraft and demonic forces.

review: 7.99/10

This movie is genuinely scary and keeps you on edge the whole time. The tension builds really well, and the creepy moments hit hard without feeling over-the-top. The story is engaging, the characters feel real, and the atmosphere is seriously unsettling. Definitely one of the best haunted house horror movies out there.

The conjuring 2

The Warrens travel to London to investigate the Enfield poltergeist, where a single mother and her children are plagued by violent supernatural activity, revealing shocking truths about possession.

review: 7.67/10

This one’s creepy and full of tense moments that actually stick with you. The story digs deeper into the characters, which makes it more than just scares. Some parts slow down, but the atmosphere is strong, and the haunting scenes are genuinely unsettling. A solid, spooky watch.

The conjuring 3

The Warrens investigate a murder case where the accused claims demonic possession. They uncover a sinister entity that manipulates humans to commit heinous acts.

review: 9.11/10

This one’s darker and more intense than the first two, with plenty of tense and creepy moments. The story is more focused on mystery and investigation, which makes it feel gripping instead of just scary for the sake of it. Some parts drag a bit, but the suspense and atmosphere keep it interesting. Definitely worth a watch if you like horror with a strong story.
