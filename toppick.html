Here are my top favorites & recommendations

IT

A group of kids in a small town face their worst fears when a terrifying clown, Pennywise, starts haunting them. Together, they must fight to survive and stop the evil.

Review: 9.10/10

It is honestly one of my favorite movies. I love how it blends real horror with the strong friendship of the kids. Pennywise is terrifying, but the story has so much heart that it sticks with me long after watching. It’s dark, emotional, and unforgettable—I could watch it again and again.

The Substance

A rising pop star is haunted by a terrifying curse that spreads through smiles. She must face the horror before it destroys her life and everyone around her.

Review: 9.74/10

The Substance really surprised me, and its easily one of my favorite horror movies. The story about wanting to be young again and heartbreaking, but its also filled with shocking, moments that stay in your mind. Demi Moore and Margaret Qualley were amazing they made the characters feel alive and emotional. Its gross at times, but thats what makes it powerful. Its the kind of movie you keep thinking about even after it ends.

Smile 2

A fading actress uses a mysterious drug that creates a younger version of herself, but the price of beauty turns into a horrifying nightmare.

Review: 9.30/10

Smile 2 quickly became one of my favorites. It’s creepy, unsettling, and keeps you on edge from start to finish. The scares feel intense, but what really makes it stand out is how it plays with fear and emotion at the same time. It’s the kind of horror that stays in your head long after the credits roll, and that’s why I love it.

Train to Busan

Passengers on a speeding train try to escape a sudden zombie outbreak, but survival comes at a heartbreaking cost as chaos and fear take over.

Review: 9.89/10

Train to Busan is a rollercoaster of scares and emotions. The zombie action is nonstop, but what really hits you are the human moments people fighting to survive, protect their loved ones, and make impossible choices. Its thrilling, heartbreaking, and stays with you long after it ends.

Carrie

A shy high school girl discovers she has terrifying powers, and when bullying pushes her too far, the quest for revenge turns into a nightmarish tragedy.

Review: 9.67/10

Carrie really stuck with me. It’s haunting, emotional, and keeps you on edge the whole time. The scares are intense, but what hits hardest is how it mixes heartbreak and horror;something that stays with you long after its over.

A Quiet Place

A family must live in complete silence to survive deadly creatures that hunt by sound, but every small mistake turns survival into a tense and terrifying ordeal.

Review: 9.78/10

A Quiet Place is nervewracking and surprisingly emotional. Every tiny sound feels like it could be deadly, keeping you completely on edge, but at the same time, you cant help caring about the family fighting to survive. Its scary, heartfelt, and stays with you long after its over.
